What is BeMyVoice?

BeMyVoice is an innovative application designed to enhance the quality of life for deaf students by addressing key challenges they face in their daily routines. The app features Sign Language Recognition, which employs advanced machine learning models to facilitate real-time translation between spoken language and sign language, ensuring clear communication. Additionally, it offers Interactive Learning Tools that utilize curated datasets and technology to provide engaging educational resources for learning sign language, fostering a more inclusive learning environment.

Furthermore, BeMyVoice incorporates Indoor Navigation Assistance, leveraging augmented reality (AR) to help users navigate unfamiliar indoor spaces with ease and confidence. To ensure user safety, the app includes a Vibration-based Danger Detection system that employs dual CNN models to identify hazardous situations and alert users promptly. Together, these components create a comprehensive platform that empowers deaf students and fosters a supportive community where they can connect and share their experiences.

Component 1

Communication Assistance

Our system authenticates gestures using two sequential Random Forest models. First, a binary classification model identifies gestures as either 'signing' or 'non-signing.' If a gesture is recognized as 'signing,' the relevant frame, along with preceding and succeeding frames, is resized to 48x48 pixels and analyzed by a second Random Forest model. This model detects nuanced features, distinguishing 'spontaneous' from 'posed' expressions, which enhances gesture recognition accuracy. Additionally, the system tracks the average proportions of signing versus non-signing actions, offering insights into sign language usage patterns.

The app enables inclusive one-on-one online video meetings, translating spoken voice to sign language in real-time for seamless interaction between deaf and hearing participants. With a bidirectional sign language and voice translation engine, it fosters accessible communication, ensuring clarity and inclusivity for all users.

Component 2

Sign Language Learning Tools platform

The Interactive Learning Tools component leverages advanced technology to create an engaging educational experience tailored for deaf students. Curated datasets, including 'Sign Language MNIST' and custom video clips, provide a solid foundation. Through preprocessing with tools like OpenCV and MediaPipe, video data is segmented into frames and labeled with corresponding gestures to ensure thorough educational content.Our app offers real-time sign language interpretation of spoken audio from online educational videos, removing the need for pre-translated content and allowing deaf and hard-of-hearing students unlimited access to educational resources. Additionally, an intelligent chatbot uses sign language to guide students through the platform, helping them locate learning materials and relevant courses in a supportive, sign language-friendly environment.

Component 3

Navigation Assistance

The Indoor Navigation Assistance system leverages Augmented Reality (AR) technology to deliver an intuitive navigation experience within buildings. By integrating detailed indoor maps and landmark identifiers with AR capabilities, users can view navigation instructions overlaid on their real-world environment in real-time. CNN models enable accurate landmark detection and navigation guidance, using ARCore and directional arrows, points of interest, and other cues directly in the user’s view through their mobile device. The system’s integration with Google Maps through the Maps SDK for Android enhances accuracy by combining indoor navigation paths, providing users with a comprehensive navigation experience.

This AR-powered approach not only boosts user orientation and spatial awareness but also turns navigation into an engaging, interactive experience, helping users confidently explore complex indoor environments.

Component 4

Safety and Alert Systems

The Vibration-based Danger Detection system enhances user safety through a dual CNN approach. Initially, a LeNet-based CNN model performs binary classification to distinguish 'safe' from 'dangerous' scenarios. If danger is detected, a more advanced CNN model, combining ResNet-18 and convLSTM, further analyzes specific indicators like sirens or alarms. The system instantly triggers vibrations to alert the user and records the frequency of hazardous situations over time, providing valuable insights into environmental safety. The Student Locator system allows a deaf student to share their location and a message with their caregiver’s mobile device in an emergency. Leveraging the Google Maps API for accurate GPS-based location sharing, it verifies the sender’s identity through packet transfer to the correct IP address. Location details, including latitude and longitude, are encrypted to ensure the student’s privacy.
